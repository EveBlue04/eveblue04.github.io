NAU-CHINA's project "Temperature-Controlled One-step Synthesis of Colored Bacterial Cellulose" addresses environmental concerns in the textile industry through innovative synthetic biology approaches. Our research integrates microbial metabolic engineering with traditional textile principles to create sustainable materials.

Cultural Connection

Our project draws profound inspiration from China's ancient silk traditions. The "weaving and dyeing in one pot" concept directly references historical Chinese textile techniques where artisans achieved remarkable efficiency through integrated processes.

SHANGHAI_Tech's project focuses on developing biosensors for detecting heavy metals in traditional Chinese medicine preparations. Our approach combines synthetic biology with quality control methodologies essential for preserving medicinal efficacy.

Using engineered bacterial strains that produce visible color changes in the presence of contaminants, we've created a rapid, field-deployable testing system that maintains the integrity of traditional formulations while ensuring safety standards.

Cultural Connection

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) has relied on meticulous quality assessment for millennia, with practitioners using sensory evaluation to determine herb potency. Our biosensor technology modernizes this ancient practice of "observation and verification" that forms the foundation of TCM diagnostics.
